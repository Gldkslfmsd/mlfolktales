Story Title: The Fox and the Stork
Story ID (from ATU-website): 7963
ATU Classification Number: 60

One should do injury to none;But he that has th' assault begun,Ought, says the fabulist, to findThe dread of being served in kind,A Fox, to sup within his caveThe Stork an invitation gave,Where, in a shallow dish, was pour'dSome broth, which he himself devoured;While the poor hungry Stork was fainInevitably to abstain.The Stork, in turn, the Fox invites,And brings her liver and her lightsIn a tall flagon, finely minced,And thrusting in her beak, convincedThe Fox that he in grief must fast,While she enjoy'd the rich repast.Then, as in vain he lick'd the neck,The Stork was heard her guest to check, -' That every one the fruits should bearOf their example, is but fair."
