Story Title: Esop and the Importunate Fellow
Story ID (from ATU-website): 7983
ATU Classification Number: UNKNOWN

Esop (no other slave at hand)Received himself his lord's commandAn early supper to provide.From house to house he therefore triedTo beg the favor of a light;At length he hit upon the right.But as when first he sallied outHe made his tour quite round about,On his return he took a raceDirectly, cross the market-place:When thus a talkative buffoon," Esop, what means this light at noon ?'He answer'd briefly, as he ran,"Fellow, I'm looking for a man."Now if this jackanapes had weighedThe true intent of what was said,He'd found that Esop had no senseOf manhood in impertinence.
